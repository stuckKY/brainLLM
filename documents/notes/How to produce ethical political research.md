#university #research_project


- Ethical research is professional practice undertaken in accordance with a code of ethics, which governs the way research is conducted
- Ethical issues have become increasingly important in guiding research, and researchers must be aware of these to conduct professional and responsible research
- Importance of ethical scrutiny has increased over time - most universities now have ethics committes
- Designed to protect participants from exploitation or harm and protect the university from potential legal action or unwanted publicity.

#### Participant protection
- Need to:
	- Seek informed consent, with the participants being told about the purpose and likely benefits of the study, as well as any risks involved.
	- Maintain confidentiality, with participants having the right to anonymity and privacy
	- Ensure research does not have the possibility of causing harm, it is generally regarded as unacceptable

#### Avoiding bias with sponsors
- Have to report findings accurately and unbiasedly, using information in a way that does not affect participants adversely
- Sponsoring organisations will also have ethical considerations, including restrictions imposed on research designs and the possible use of findings
- Misuse of information by sponsoring organisations is also a concern.
- Researchers making evidence-based knowledge claims should reference the data they have used and provide access to those data or explain why they cannot
- Should offer a full account of the procedures used to collect or generate the data and provide a full account of how analytical conclusions have been drawn
