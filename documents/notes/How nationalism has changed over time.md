#### Origins of Nationalism
- Some argue it emerges out of the social transformations of 17th and 18th century Europe
- Others, like Ernest Gellner say it comes from the transformation from agricultural to industrial, which alters the relationship between culture and politics and necessitates a change in the place of culture in social organisation.
- This isn't too dissimilar to the **modernisation thesis**
	- Nationalism emerges as a result of modernisation and industrialisation
	- Many - especially Marxists - have adopted this approach
	- But it could imply the era of nationalsim has passed, which is not supported by current nationalist or ethnic conflicts
	- And it doesn't tell us much about how nationalism works as a political ideology

#### Nationalism and Ethnicity
- Anthony Smith stresses the necessity of a prior 'ethnic core' for modern nationalism
	- Argues that nations have roots going much further back than the emergence of nationalism as an ideology
	- Emphasises importance of myths, collective memories, persistent traditions and shared symbols
- The formation of a nation is a long process and there is no single point when it turns into a nation
