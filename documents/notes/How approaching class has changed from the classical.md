Challenges to classical class analysis
- Changing class structures: decline of industrial working class, expansion of female employment
	- The things that defined class position mainly applied to male workers, not most people in society. 
- Political changes: political salience of class (decline of traditional left, rise of NSM)
	- There is a rise with the labour party, but then a decline later in the 20th century. NSM is New Social Movements, which are interested in other forms of identity - gender, sexuality, environmental activism etc.
- The death of class analysis; end of class society - individualisation - new concepts to study new social forms (Beck)
	- In the 19th centuries, there are societies where people conduct themselves as groups, but in the late 20th century you get an increasing concept of individualisation. Beck says class analysis is only appropriate for certain periods.
- Political opposition to class
	- *'Class is a Communist concept. It groups people as bundles and sets them against one another. I remember practically exploding when I heard some Americans talking about 'the underclass' as if they weren't individuals with feelings. ... Underclass? Socialist claptrap!... The more you talk about class - or even about classlessness - the more you fix the idea in people's minds.'* (Thatcher, 1992)
	- Thatcher also spoke about the need for 'the nation' than class.
