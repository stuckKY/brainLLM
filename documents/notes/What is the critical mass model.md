- Refers to a self-sustaining activity that occurs when a certain minimum level of participation is reached
- Often used to describe social phenomena where individual behaviour is influenced by the actions of others
- Lemons model: Seller of a used car knows whether it is a "lemon" or not, while the buyer can only play the averages, leading to a situation where the market may disappear - unequal information can lead to market failure
- Important to see the influence of **absolute numbers** versus proportions
	- If absolute matter, an activity may be self-sustaining in a large group but not a small one
	- If proportions matter, there is the possibility of dividing or separating proportions, which can affect the likelihood of reaching critical mass
- Individuals make decisions based on the actions of others
	- May be attracted to the majority - volunteer if a certain number of others do, or stay in a neighbourhood if a certain number of others remain
- The critical mass model **does not necessarily** determine the prefered outcome - observed outcome may not be unanimously prefered
	- Can result in multiple sustainable outcomes, and the introduction of unconditional participants can influence the behaviour of conditional participants, leading to a single ineluctable outcome
