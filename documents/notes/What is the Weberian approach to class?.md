Max Weber differentiated 'Class' and 'Status' - with both being important, but in different ways. In his view, they are very often conflated.
	- Class is an individual's position in the market, e.g. their occupations
	- Status is an individual's position in society
- What differentiates people of different classes is actually their different statuses - social networks, who you know. There are occupational groups too.
- Weber was interested in class structures but made the point that people would sometimes become conscious of commonality in occupation, but not always the basis for action.
	- He said classes are 'not communities, merely possible, and frequent basis for communal action'.
	- Status groups meanwhile, usually are communities. Status provides the most frequent basis for common action.
- In terms of modern developments, there are more active researchers in the Weberian route. 
	- Goldthorpe developed an approach where the class structure is based on work position. He systematically worked on the class side of the Weber's scheme.
	- He then made the connections of the relationship between class structures and consciousness, plus the relationship between class consciousness and class action. A lot of previous work had put it together into one chain but he split them up to understand separately.
