#### Historical analysis of class
- Must be sensitive to the constructed nature of social visions of class 
**Runciman** and **McKibbin** have made significant contributions to the historiography of modern Britain, offering subtle readings of c20th English social history
- But limitations and omissions in their accounts
	- McKibbin relies on retrospective accounts of working-class life and lacks attention to the heterogeneity of working-class culture
- The concept of "working class culture" as a tool of historical analysis should be questioned, and the complexities of class identity and cultural practices should be further explored
