**Cultural turn**
- Cultural Turn 1: Ethnography of class - no interest in questions of class action
	- Trying to understand the experiences of people in different class positions. It's an interest in class consciousness without an interest in structures or what it results in with action.
	- Analysing a class in its own terms, not for anything else
- Cultural Turn 2: Bourdieu: Three types of capital (economic, social, cultural) in different combinations (different)
	- Bourdieu said class had been excessively economic. He suggested class could be understood much better bringing in his three types of capital.
	- You can get people with economic capital but very little cultural capital - e.g. 'do you go to the opera?'. People with high-status cultural tastes have high levels of cultural capital. You can also have that with low economic capital - many students for example.
- Broad approach behind much contemporary sociological research into class
