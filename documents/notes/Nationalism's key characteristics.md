- Some scholars emphasise its objective features, such as shared language, territory and historical conditions
- Other focus on its subjective aspects like belief, commitment and imagination
- Historian **Hugh Secton-Watson** defines a nation as existing when a significant number of people in a community consider themselves to form a nation or behave as if they formed one.
- **Ernest Renan** views it as a spiritual principle, constituted by a shared legacy of remembrances and the desire to live together
#### Characteristics of nationalism
- A belief in national self-determination and the view that nations are a natural unit of social and political organisation
- But not necessarily universal and instead a particularistic concern with one's own nation
- Also marked by strong emotionalism and attachment to a particular territory and history
- It is peculiar among ideologies in that its subjective and emotional aspects are foregrounded
- Provides a sense of who we are and makes us proud about it - it is a way of being within the world, giving us a sense of what is going on and organising our perceptions
